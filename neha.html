Art as Therapy: Healing Through Creation

Ravi Kumar

Art Therapist

When words failed me, art spoke. My journey from darkness to light, one brushstroke at a time.

Two years ago, I was battling severe depression. Medication helped, therapy helped, but something was still missing. Then a friend gifted me a basic painting kit and suggested I try creating something—anything.

I had no formal training, no artistic background. But I started with simple patterns, inspired by the Indian folk art I grew up seeing around me. The act of focusing on creating something, of watching colors blend and forms emerge, became my daily meditation.

What started as therapy evolved into a passion. I discovered mandala art, learned about the meditative practice of creating intricate geometric patterns. Each circle I drew, each petal I colored, was a step away from anxiety and toward peace.

I joined local art groups, took workshops on traditional Indian art forms, connected with artisans who generously shared their knowledge. I found a community of healers and creators who understood that art is not just about the final product—it is about the journey of creation itself.

Now, I conduct art therapy workshops for people dealing with mental health challenges. We use various Indian art forms—Mandala, Zentangle inspired by Madhubani, simple pottery—anything that allows people to express without words.

Art saved my life. Not in a dramatic, overnight way, but slowly, gently, one brushstroke at a time. It gave me purpose, community, and a way to transform pain into beauty.

If you are struggling, pick up a brush, some clay, some colored pencils. You do not need to be good at it. You just need to start.
